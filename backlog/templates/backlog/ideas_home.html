Prioritizing your backlog

No team has infinite time and resources at their disposal. We all have to make difficult decisions about how to prioritize our finite resources in order to deliver the most value to our stakeholders.

We know user feedback is critical to building a successful product, but gathering this feedback and aggregating it in an objective manner can be difficult. One of the most challenging aspects of digesting the vast amount of inputs we receive, is reconciling conflicting data points.

In my experience, letting our users make direct comparisons themselves and then aggregating those opinions across our userbase, has proved to be a successful technique. Below I have two methods for gathering direct user feedback in a way that allows easy aggregation.

First, we have the "Pairwise Comparison." Utilizing the ranking system used by Chess masters, we force users to choose between two features/ideas, and then rank the items based on their "performance."

Second, is what I call the sampled ranking. Here I take 10 random ideas and have users drag them into the order of preference.
